Based on the above diagram, it seems that for short window lengths (L = 256 or L = 512), there is no satisfactory approximation of the spectrum since as observed, compared to large window lengths, not all peaks and valleys are shown as we increase L. Also, the discrimination for small L increases, although

the function becomes smooth, we do not seek it to estimate the spectrum. So it makes sense to go for long window lengths.

Comparing the curves for L = 1024 and for L = 2048 there is a strong stochasticity of the spectrum estimation, when L = 2048, due to the large oscillating behavior. So, although for L = 2048 we have less resolution than for L = 1024, we should not choose window length 2048 because there is a lot of

thoughtfulness in estimating the error. Finally, in order to have a small distinction that helps us in the estimation, but also in order not to have a lot of contemplation, L = 1024 is chosen as the appropriate window length.
